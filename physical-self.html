Factors or Aspects that Describe the Physical Self

Physical Self refers to the body, this marvelous container and complex, finely tuned, machine with which we interface with our environment and fellow beings. The Physical Self is the concrete dimension, the tangible aspect of the person that can be directly observed and examined.


  • Physical appearance - The outward appearance of any person, irrespective of sex, with regard to hair style, beards, manner of dress, weight, height, facial features, or other aspects of appearance.
  • Body image - The perception that a person has of their physical self and the thoughts and feelings that result from that perception. These feelings can be positive, negative or both, and are influenced by individual and environmental factors.
  • Physical fitness and abilities - Physical fitness refers to the ability of your body systems to work together efficiently to allow you to be healthy and perform activities of daily living. Being efficient means doing daily activities with the least effort possible.
  • Personal grooming and self-care - Grooming plays a major role in maintaining a high self-esteem and self-confidence. It does this by influencing your appearance, which in turn affects the way you regard yourself. When your hair looks neat and beautiful, and your gel manicure is still intact, you can observe how your confidence will rise.
  • Physical abilities and achievements - This aspect encompasses an individual's accomplishments, skills, and performance in physical activities, sports, or hobbies. It includes accomplishments like finishing a marathon, becoming an expert at a new yoga posture, or dominating a certain sport. Physical abilities and achievements may enhance self-confidence and one's image of their physical selves.

College Students' Challenges and Issues with their Physical Self


  • Poor health habits: College students often struggle to maintain healthy habits due to the demands of their academic and social life. Irregular sleep patterns, unhealthy eating habits, lack of exercise, and excessive stress can negatively impact their physical well-being.
  • Sedentary lifestyle: College life often involves long hours of sitting in classrooms, studying, or working on assignments. This sedentary lifestyle can lead to issues such as weight gain, decreased cardiovascular fitness, and musculoskeletal problems.
  • Limited access to nutritious food: Many college students live on campus or in shared accommodations, where access to healthy and nutritious food options may be limited. This can result in a reliance on processed or fast food, leading to inadequate nutrition and potential health problems.
  • Mental health impact: Physical well-being is closely intertwined with mental health. College students may experience stress, anxiety, or depression, which can affect their motivation to engage in physical activities or take care of their physical health.
  • Lack of time management: Balancing academic commitments, social life, extracurricular activities, and part-time jobs can make it challenging for college students to prioritize their physical well-being. Limited time may lead to neglecting exercise, proper nutrition, or self-care practices.
  • Body image issues: College can be a time when students become more self-aware and may face body image issues or concerns. Societal pressures, comparison to peers, or unrealistic beauty standards can contribute to body dissatisfaction and negative self-perception.
  • Substance abuse: Some college students may engage in substance abuse, including alcohol or drug misuse, which can have severe physical consequences and negatively impact overall wellbeing.
  • Lack of knowledge or resources: College students may not have access to reliable information about maintaining physical health, exercise routines, nutrition, or available resources on campus. This lack of knowledge or awareness can hinder their ability to make informed choices about their physical well-being.

Physical Self Contribution to Overall Understanding of The Self


  • Santrock (2014) said that self-understanding is the individual’s cognitive representation of the self which consists of the substance and the concept of self-conceptions.
  • Physical self is not just about how you look, not just about abs and such. It is also about how you nourish your self-image, self-concept and self-esteem. Physical characteristics are the defining traits of the person’s body.
  • The physical self contributes to the entire concept of the self by influencing one's self-image, serving as a foundation for embodiment and identity expression, affecting physical health and well-being, promoting body awareness, and impacting sensory experiences. It is crucial for how people view themselves, engage with the outside world, and form a feeling of authenticity and identity.

Tips and Advice on How to Manage and Take Care of Physical Self

For general well-being and academic success in college, taking care of your physical needs is essential. To assist you in maintaining a healthy lifestyle, consider the following suggestions:


  • Prioritize regular exercise - Take up an enjoyable kind of exercise, such as walking, running, cycling, or joining a sports team.
  • Maintain a balanced diet - Eat a range of nutrient-rich foods, such as fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean meats, and healthy fats, to maintain a balanced diet. Limit your consumption of processed meals, fast food, and sugary snacks.
  • Stay hydrated - Throughout the day, consume enough water. Keep yourself hydrated by carrying a reusable water bottle with you, especially during courses and study sessions.
  • Maintain Stress - College life may be tough, so learn effective stress management techniques.
  • Use relaxation methods -like yoga, meditation, and deep breathing. Play games you like to relax, and take pauses as necessary.
  • Practice good hygiene - Maintain proper hygiene by routinely taking a shower, brushing your teeth, and cleaning your hands. To encourage a healthy atmosphere, keep your living place neat and tidy.
  • Take care of your mental health - College may be emotionally challenging, so do your best to look after it. When necessary, seek assistance from friends, family, or school resources. Engage in selfcare practices such as writing, hobbies, or outdoor activities.
