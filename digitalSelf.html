ASPECTS THAT DESCRIBE THE DIGITAL SELF

Zhao (2005) discusses digital self-construction and emphasizes the role of online interactions. She argues that the digital self is formed without the influence of non-verbal feedback and traditional environmental factors. The existence of a “digital self” does not mean that the self is divided into physical and digital parts, but the emphasis is on the “E-Audience”. This “E-Audience” witnesses us on the ‘Front stage’ of the internet, like in the form of social media profiles, when we post and interact (Zhao, 2005).

SELF PRESENTATION STRATEGIES AND IMPRESSION MANAGEMENT

1. Self-Promotion - refers to the practice of purposefully trying to present oneself ast highly coompetent to other people.

2. Ingratiation- refers to behaviors that a person illicitly enacts to make others like him or her or think well of his or her qualities as a person.

3. Exemplification- is defined as strategic self presentational strategy whereby an individual attempts to project an image of integrity and moral worthiness.

4. Intimidation- is intentional behavior that would cause a person of ordinary sensibilities to fear injury or harm.

5. Supplication- strategy for self-presentation that involves depicting oneself as weak, needy, or dependent so as to motivate others to provide assistance or care.

The term "digital self" refers to the online representation or identity of an individual. It is the persona that people create and maintain in the digital realm through various online platforms, websites, and social media. This digital self is distinct from one's physical self, but it can have a significant impact on one's life, relationships, and reputation.


The digital self can encompass a wide range of aspects, including:

1. SOCIAL MEDIA PROFILES

The collection of information and interactions an individual has on platforms like Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, LinkedIn, etc.

2. ONLINE PRESENCE

Any content related to a person that can be found online, such as blog posts, articles, videos, comments, and reviews.

3. VIRTUAL AVATARS

In the context of online games and vr, people often create avatars that represent them and reflecting their aspects of digital self.

4. DIGITAL FOOTPRINT

The trail of data and information that individuals leave behind as they use the internet and interact with online services.

5. ONLINE BEHAVIOR

The way people interact with others, express themselves, and present their opinions and beliefs on various online platforms.

6. Privacy and Security

The level of control individuals have over their personal information and how they safeguard their digital selves from potential risks.

The digital self can bring both advantages and disadvantages. It enables global connections, idea sharing, and professional networking, but also entails risks like privacy concerns, online harassment, and permanent digital footprints. To navigate the digital world responsibly, individuals should be mindful of their online presence, exercise caution with shared information, utilize privacy settings, and consider the impact of their online actions. Being genuine online can foster more meaningful connections and interactions with others.


Life in the digital self refers to how individuals navigate and experience their existence through the lens of their online presence and interactions. In today's interconnected world, many aspects of our lives have become intertwined with the digital realm. Here are some key aspects of life in the digital self:

1. **Social Media and Relationships:** Social media platforms play a significant role in shaping our social lives. People use platforms like Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, and Snapchat to connect with friends, family, and acquaintances, share updates about their lives, and stay informed about others.

2. **Online Communication:** Digital self has revolutionized the way we communicate. Email, instant messaging, video calls, and social media messaging have become integral to both personal and professional interactions.

3. **Personal Branding and Identity:** The digital self allows individuals to create and curate their personal brand and identity. People often share specific aspects of their lives and interests to present themselves in a certain way to their online audience.

4. **Online Communities and Support Networks:** The internet has enabled the formation of online communities based on shared interests, hobbies, or experiences. These communities can provide support, encouragement, and a sense of belonging.

5. **E-commerce and Online Shopping:** Digital technology has transformed the way we shop and consume goods and services. Online shopping platforms have made it easier to make purchases from the comfort of one's home.

6. **Digital Entertainment and Media Consumption:** Streaming services, online gaming, and digital content platforms have become prevalent forms of entertainment, allowing people to consume media at their convenience.

7. **Remote Work and Education:** The digital self has also affected the way we work and learn. Remote work and online education have become more common, allowing people to connect and collaborate regardless of physical location.

8. **Privacy and Security Concerns:** As individuals lead much of their lives online, there are growing concerns about privacy and data security. People need to be vigilant about protecting their personal information and being aware of potential risks.

9. **Mental Health and Well-being:** The constant presence of the digital self can impact mental health. Online experiences, such as social media comparisons and cyberbullying, can influence self-esteem and overall well-being.

10. **Digital Legacy:** The digital self raises questions about one's digital legacy, the information and content that will remain after a person passes away.

The concept of "self in digital" refers to how individuals perceive, represent, and express themselves within the context of the digital world. It encompasses the digital self as discussed earlier, but it also emphasizes the subjective and personal aspects of one's identity in the online space. Here are some key points to consider when exploring the self in digital:

1. **Identity and Representation:** In the digital realm, individuals can construct and shape their identity by choosing what to share and how to present themselves online. This representation may differ from their offline self, and people often curate their online presence to align with specific interests or goals.

2. **Multiple Digital Identities:** Many individuals maintain various digital identities across different platforms or online communities. Each identity may highlight different aspects of their personality or cater to specific social circles or interests.

3. **Anonymity and Pseudonymity:** Some online spaces allow users to remain anonymous or use pseudonyms, providing a level of privacy and freedom to express themselves without revealing their offline identity fully.

4. **Expression and Communication:** The digital environment enables various modes of expression, including text, digitalSelfimages, videos, and multimedia content. People can communicate through memes, GIFs, emojis, and other digital forms that enrich their online interactions.

5. **Social Interactions and Networks:** The self in digital heavily relies on social interactions with others. Online friendships, followers, and connections contribute to the formation of a person's digital identity and influence how they are perceived within the online community.

6. **Digital Footprint:** All online activities contribute to a person's digital footprint, which is the trail of data they leave behind. This digital trail may affect how they are perceived by others and can have implications in both personal and professional contexts.

7. **Authenticity and Fakeness:** Balancing authenticity and the desire to present an idealized version of oneself is a common challenge in the digital world. Some individuals may struggle with being authentic, fearing judgment or negative consequences.

8. **Digital Citizenship:** Being a responsible digital citizen involves respecting others' rights, being mindful of the impact of one's online actions, and contributing positively to online communities.

9. **Impact on Mental Health:** Spending significant time in the digital space can impact mental health. Online interactions, social media comparisons, and cyberbullying are some factors that can affect an individual's emotional well-being.

10. **Digital Transformation:** Over time, the digital self may evolve as technology and online platforms change. Individuals may adapt their digital presence to align with emerging trends and communication methods.
